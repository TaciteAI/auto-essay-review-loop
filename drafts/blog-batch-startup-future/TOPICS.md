# 20-Topic Plan — AI Startup Future Batch

Drafted from 2026-05-05 web research (see `SOURCE_NOTES.md`). Each blog 500–800 words, EN + CN.
Filenames `{NN}-{slug}.{en,cn}.md`.

## Moat-side (1–10) — what's defensible in 2026

1. **wrapper-era-over** — Why "ChatGPT-on-top-of-an-API" stopped being a startup the moment Operator shipped.
2. **service-as-software** — Sequoia's inversion: sell outcomes, not seats. Look like a services firm to the customer, run like a software company internally.
3. **distribution-is-the-moat** — In 2026, investors evaluate distribution before they evaluate the model. Why a great AI product with no GTM isn't fundable.
4. **data-flywheel-vs-hoard** — a16z's empty promise: volume isn't the moat. Quality, domain specificity, and a flywheel are.
5. **workflow-lockin** — The new switching cost: when the agent has been operating in your CRM for 18 months, you don't rip it out.
6. **vertical-beats-horizontal** — Why deep-in-one-field agents reach revenue faster, retain better, and earn higher gross margins.
7. **trust-and-compliance** — In healthcare, finance, legal: the moat is the audit trail, not the model. Regulators are the gate.
8. **multiplayer-agent-network-effects** — Network effects are subtler in AI but real: agents that talk to other agents in the same vertical compound.
9. **system-of-action** — Why owning the action layer beats owning the record layer. Salesforce's record, the agent's action.
10. **brand-and-ux-when-capability-is-rented** — When the model is a commodity, the only thing left to own is taste, voice, and interface.

## Trend-side (11–20) — what's actually happening

11. **death-of-prompt-box** — a16z: AI applications stop waiting for instructions and start observing/intervening. The chat interface is a transitional artifact.
12. **outcome-pricing** — Agents priced per closed deal, per resolved ticket, per filed brief — not per seat. The pricing model is the product.
13. **vendor-consolidation** — Mature buyers actively cutting from 50 agents to 2 per workflow. Late entrants compete against incumbents who already have the contract.
14. **155M-series-a** — Avg agentic round size nearly doubled to $155M. Fewer companies, bigger checks. What that means for the next thousand AI founders.
15. **foundation-models-as-competitors** — OpenAI Operator, Claude for Work, Gemini agents — your platform is now your competitor. How startups are responding.
16. **ai-native-hedge-fund** — YC's swarm-of-agents thesis: agents reading the entire internet, trading on semantic signals humans can't process. The first cohort of asymmetric capital.
17. **ai-native-agency** — The other YC bet: deliver finished outcomes with near-zero marginal labor. The death of the billable hour.
18. **open-vs-closed-cost-curve** — DeepSeek, Llama, Qwen, Mistral closed most of the gap. What does that buy a 2026 founder, and what doesn't it buy?
19. **vertical-saas-rebundling** — Every vertical SaaS now ships an AI layer. The new question: who rebundles around the agent — the SaaS or the agent?
20. **micro-saas-corner** — The 20 niches AI won't kill: regulated trust, embodied service, hyper-local context. The solo founder's durable angle.
